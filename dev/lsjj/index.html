LS-jj recoupling

By defination

\[\begin{aligned} \mathrm{lsjj}(l_1,j_1,l_2,j_2,L,S,J) &= \langle l_1l_2L,s_1s_2S,J|l_1s_1j_1,l_2s_2j_2,J\rangle \\ &= \begin{bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ L & S & J\end{bmatrix} \\ &= \sqrt{(2j_1+1)(2j_2+1)(2L+1)(2S+1)}\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ L & S & J\end{Bmatrix}. \end{aligned}\]

We only deal with $s_1 = s_2 = 1/2$ condition.

$S = 0$

Ref [1] P357, 10.9.1, (1).

\[\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J & 0 & J\end{Bmatrix} = \dfrac{(-1)^{l_1+s_1+j_2+J}}{\sqrt{2(2J+1)}} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}.\]

So

\[\begin{bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J & 0 & J\end{bmatrix} = (-1)^{l_1+s_1+j_2+J}\sqrt{\dfrac{(2j_1+1)(2j_2+1)}{2}} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}.\]

Here we define

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) \equiv (-1)^{l_1+\frac{1}{2}+j_2+J}\sqrt{\dfrac{(2j_1+1)(2j_2+1)}{2}} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}.\]

It is useful in laster formula.

$S = 1$

$L = J$

Ref [1] P358, 10.9.2, (6).

\[\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J & 1 & J\end{Bmatrix} = (-1)^{l_1+s_1+j_2+J}\frac{(j_1-j_2)(j_1+j_2+1) - (l_1-l_2)(l_1+l_2+1)}{\sqrt{6J(J+1)(2J+1)}}\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}.\]

So

\[\begin{bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J & 1 & J\end{bmatrix} = \frac{(j_1-j_2)(j_1+j_2+1) - (l_1-l_2)(l_1+l_2+1)}{\sqrt{J(J+1)}} H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2).\]

$J = L + 1$

Ref [1] P358, 10.9.2, (7).

\[\begin{aligned} &(-1)^{l_1+s_1+j_2+J-1}\sqrt{6J(2J-1)(2J+1)}\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J-1 & 1 & J\end{Bmatrix} \\ =& \sqrt{(j_1-j_2+J)(j_2-j_1+J)(j_1+j_2+J+1)(j_1+j_2-J+1)}\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J-1 & l_2\end{Bmatrix} \\ &+ \sqrt{(l_1-l_2+J)(l_2-l_1+J)(l_1+l_2+J+1)(l_1+l_2-J+1)} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}. \end{aligned}\]

So

\[\begin{aligned} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J-1 & 1 & J\end{Bmatrix} &= \sqrt{\frac{(j_1-j_2+J)(j_2-j_1+J)(j_1+j_2+J+1)(j_1+j_2-J+1)}{J(2J+1)}}H_{J-1}(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) \\ &-\sqrt{\frac{(l_1-l_2+J)(l_2-l_1+J)(l_1+l_2+J+1)(l_1+l_2-J+1)}{J(2J+1)}} H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2). \end{aligned}\]

$J = L - 1$

Ref [1] P358, 10.9.2, (7).

\[\begin{aligned} &(-1)^{l_1+s_1+j_2+J}\sqrt{6J(2J+1)(2J+3)}\begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J-1 & 1 & J\end{Bmatrix} \\ =&\sqrt{(j_1-j_2+L)(j_2-j_1+L)(j_1+j_2+L+1)(j_1+j_2-L+1)} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J+1 & l_2\end{Bmatrix} \\ &+\sqrt{(l_1-l_2+L)(l_2-l_1+L)(l_1+l_2+L+1)(l_1+l_2-L+1)} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & \frac{1}{2} & j_1 \\ j_2 & J & l_2\end{Bmatrix}. \end{aligned}\]

So

\[\begin{aligned} \begin{Bmatrix}l_1 & s_1 & j_1 \\ l_2 & s_2 & j_2 \\ J+1 & 1 & J\end{Bmatrix} &=\sqrt{\frac{(l_1-l_2+L)(l_2-l_1+L)(l_1+l_2+L+1)(l_1+l_2-L+1)}{(J+1)(2J+1)}} H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) \\ &- \sqrt{\frac{(j_1-j_2+L)(j_2-j_1+L)(j_1+j_2+L+1)(j_1+j_2-L+1)}{(J+1)(2J+1)}}H_{J+1}(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2). \end{aligned}\]

$H$ function

According to the symmetry of Wigner-6j symbol, one can find

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) = (-1)^{l_1+l_2+j_1+j_2+1} H_{J}(l_2,l_1,j_2,j_1).\]

  • \[j_1 = l_1 + \frac12,\; j_2 = l_2 + \frac12\]

Ref [1] P300, 9.5.2, (5).

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) = \sqrt{\dfrac{(j_1+j_2+J+1)(j_1+j_2-J)}{8j_1j_2}}.\]

  • \[j_1 = l_1 + \frac12,\; j_2 = l_2 - \frac12\]

Ref [1] P300, 9.5.2, (4).

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) = \sqrt{\dfrac{(j_1-j_2+J)(j_2-j_1+J+1)}{8j_1(j_2+1)}}.\]

  • \[j_1 = l_1 - \frac12,\; j_2 = l_2 + \frac12\]

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) = -\sqrt{\dfrac{(j_1-j_2+J+1)(j_2-j_1+J)}{8(j_1+1)j_2}}.\]

  • \[j_1 = l_1 - \frac12,\; j_2 = l_2 - \frac12\]

\[H_J(l_1,l_2,j_1,j_2) = \sqrt{\dfrac{(j_1+j_2+J+2)(j_1+j_2-J+1)}{8(j_1+1)(j_2+1)}}.\]

  • 1A. N. Moskalev D. A. Varshalovich and V. K. Khersonskii, Quantum theory of angular momentum.
